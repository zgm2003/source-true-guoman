# Source-Safe Physicalization

Use source-safe physicalization to make shots feel embodied without changing source events, dialogue, or posture.

## Hard Boundary

- Keep exact source dialogue and posture unchanged.
- main motion requires source support: do not invent a new primary action unless the source states or clearly implies it.
- secondary animation must be consequence: small motion can only follow from a sourced posture, emotion, line delivery, contact, weather, lighting, or existing environment.
- must not add standing, walking, kneeling, bowing, weapon drawing, attacks, prop raising, prop putting away, seat changes, or exits unless the source directly supports that action.

## Safe Detail Fields

- facial micro-performance: use eyelids, brows, lips, throat movement, swallowed pauses, stillness, tiny gaze changes, or controlled breath to sharpen emotion.
- breath/cloth/hair/environment: add subtle breath, sleeve settling, hair tips shifting, dust, fog, light flicker, shadow movement, sound cues, or ambient pressure when they follow the scene state.
- Comedy contrast can come from restrained reaction timing, awkward stillness, over-serious framing of a small beat, or environmental sound undercutting a line; it must not rewrite the event.

## Unsafe Escalation

- unsafe motion escalation turns a micro-expression into unsourced blocking, travel, combat, kneeling, bowing, prop handling, seat changes, or exits.
- Unsafe examples: a seated character rises only to make a line more dramatic; a hand raises a cup, weapon, seal, fan, or talisman that the source did not mention; a character crosses the room to create depth; a reaction shot adds an attack, stumble, kneel, or exit.
- If source support is unclear, keep the body anchored and use face, breath, cloth, hair, dust, fog, light, sound, or comedy contrast instead.
